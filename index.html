A Novel for the most part is just like a Light Novel, the main sctick of a Novel though is that it is significantly longer per volume and usually uses more complicated words.

it is not something that aims to be a quick and easy read while travelling compared to Light Novels. Another thing about Novels is the lack of images or illustrations that help readers visualize, it relies more on the readers imaginations. It isn't all bad though as Novels have 1 thing Light novels do not do. They tell the reader who says what at a given time.

Mushoku Tensei is one of my most favourite Light Novels as it was one of my first Light Novels that I have read. It depicts a middle aged man who wasted his life exploiting his parents kindness until they died, to which he was kicked out by his siblings before dying and getting a second chance at life. The entire story is him from when he was born all the way till he dies from old age as he does his best living life to the fullest and leaving no regrets.

It isn't the main reason why I like it but instead him encountering past life traumas and showing growth as he overcomes them in his new life, but it isn't all ups and instead also has downs as he himself was immature and it lead to the deaths of his loved ones, showing despair and strength as he carries his mistakes and improves on himself with the support of those around him.

Re:Monster while not liked as much as Mushoku Tensei, it's still one of the best for me. It was also actually my first Light Novel even if it never got me into Light Novels.

Web novels have existed since the internet became widespread. It was a way that let many authors aspiring or professionals to publish their work to a wider audience.

One popular site for authors to post their work in japan would be "Shōsetsuka ni Narō" and if their work was popular enough it can get picked up by a editor from a publishing company, then if all went well the web novel would eventually become a novel to be sold for the author's to earn cash.

Money earned from being a light novel author can be as little as about $100 a month up to $500 a month. Which is not enough to live, it's not an easy job and also requires a bit of luck, so even if you are skilled enough to write breathtaking light novel stories, if it doesn't fit the trend, or it starts off slow and many others, it could lead to people dropping the series you are making and/or not travelling the people they know about it.

On the other hand, if your work gets noticed and gains traction into fame. Your salary can go up to $45,000 after tax. And even more potentially if companies want to make products/ shows/and more, netting you even more profits.

So while it may or may not be rewarding, if you do get noticed, it may be worth it especially if you enjoy writing stories.

To put things simply as a start, the key difference between a Light Novel and a Novel would be their page lengths per volume as well as Light Novels having images between, usually to illustrate the importance of the current situation that the reader is currently reading.

At the end of the whole series it felt like I was a dad watching the growth of a child becoming an independant adult that can think for himself and has people that depend on him.
